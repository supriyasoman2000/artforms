GHOOMAR

Ghoomar or ghumar is a traditional folk dance of Rajasthan. It was the Bhil tribe who performed it to worship Goddess Saraswati which was later embraced by other Rajasthani communities.The dance is chiefly performed by veiled women who wear flowing dresses called ghaghara.The dance typically involves performers pirouetting while moving in and out of a wide circle. The word ghoomna describes the twirling movement of the dancers and is the basis of the word ghoomar.According to the traditional rituals, newly married bride is expected to dance ghoomar on being welcomed to her new marital home.Ghoomar is often performed on special occasions, such as at weddings, festivals and religious occasions.which sometimes lasts for hours.
